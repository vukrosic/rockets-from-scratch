# Lesson 06: Stability, Center Of Mass, And Center Of Pressure

A rocket is not only about going upward.

It must also point the right way while moving through air.

That is the stability problem.

## Step 1: Center Of Mass

The center of mass is the balance point of the rocket's mass.

If you could balance the rocket on a finger, the balance point is near the
center of mass.

As propellant burns, the center of mass can move.

That makes rocket stability a changing problem.

## Step 2: Center Of Pressure

The center of pressure is the average point where aerodynamic forces act.

If airflow pushes on the rocket, this is where the overall aerodynamic force is
effectively located.

## Step 3: Basic Stability Idea

For passive stability, the center of pressure should usually be behind the
center of mass.

That way, if the rocket tilts, airflow tends to turn it back into the wind.

If the center of pressure is too far forward, the rocket can become unstable and
tumble.

## Step 4: Fins Help

Fins move the center of pressure backward.

That can improve stability.

We do not need design details here.

The main idea is:

```text
fins change the aerodynamic balance
```

## Step 5: Think In Terms Of Direction

Imagine the rocket tilts a little in the airflow.

If it is stable, the aerodynamic force creates a restoring tendency:

```text
tilt right -> force turns rocket back left
tilt left -> force turns rocket back right
```

That restoring behavior is what we want.

## Step 6: Simple Stability Rule

For the first model:

```text
center of mass forward
center of pressure behind it
```

This is not the only thing that matters in real flight, but it is the first
useful rule.

## Step 7: Tiny Hand Check

Question:

```text
Which should usually be in front for passive stability: center of mass or center of pressure?
```

Answer:

```text
center of mass
```

Why?

Because the aerodynamic force behind it tends to restore the pointing direction.
